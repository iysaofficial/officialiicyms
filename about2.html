Background

Indonesia is one of the countries with the largest Muslim population in the world, the large number of Muslim communities in Indonesia is expected to be a force capable of producing solutions to real problems that exist. In addition, science, especially science, is developing so rapidly. This development requires students to be able to apply the knowledge gained. Therefore, the Indonesian Young Scientist Association (IYSA) collaborates with the State Islamic University of Gunung Jati (UIN) Bandung. Organizing a competition called the International Invention Competition For Young Moslem Scientists (IICYMS). IICYMS is expected to be one of the right platforms to integrate science with religious knowledge for students both at home and abroad.
